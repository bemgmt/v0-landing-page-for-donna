### **Key Editorial Rules for the Update:**

* **Remove "Help":** Infrastructure doesn't "help"; it *supports* or *enables*. (e.g., "DONNA helps you scale" becomes "DONNA enables scale").  
* **Remove "Fun/Friendly":** Keep the tone clinical but powerful.  
* **Avoid "Chat" Terminology:** Use "Interactions," "Signals," or "Workflows" instead of "Chats" or "Messages."  
* **Emphasis on "Quiet":** Use terms like "Subtle," "Integrated," and "Behind the scenes" to reinforce the brand's "Calm" trait.

The value proposition isn't that DONNA makes a task 10% faster. It's that DONNA changes *how a real estate professional operates* — the way their day is structured, how they respond to clients, how deals move through their pipeline. That's a fundamentally different and more powerful claim.

## IBM's language around AI agents — things that *execute and adapt*, not just answer questions — is exactly what DONNA should emphasize. Real estate users don't want to prompt an AI. They want DONNA to already know that a buyer's contract expires in 3 days and surface that automatically. The framing should be: DONNA is not a tool you use, it's a layer that works *for* you in the background.

"Real estate already runs on relationships, deadlines, and coordination. DONNA is the AI layer that makes all three run automatically."

**The stacked layers diagram deserves a full section, not just an image.** That visual showing DONNA sitting in the middle of Data Sources, AI Agents, Analytics, and Knowledge Base is the single best explanation of what an "operating layer" means — and it's just floating there with no explanation. IBM would build an entire section around this. Each layer should be clickable or labeled with a one-line explanation. Right now it does the work visually but doesn't reinforce the concept verbally

**No trust/governance section.** IBM has a full pillar on sovereignty, compliance, and security. DONNA's audience — real estate agents and brokers — are deeply worried about client data, licensing compliance, and fiduciary responsibility, even if they don't say it out loud. There's a security link in the footer, but nothing on the homepage that says: *"Your client data is yours. DONNA never shares it. Everything is auditable."* 

**The chatbot CTA is a missed opportunity.** "Open the chatbot in the corner and ask DONNA" is clever but the framing is too casual. IBM positions its AI interactions as demonstrations of capability. DONNA should frame the chatbot as: *"Want to see how DONNA would handle your specific workflow? Ask it."* — making it a live proof point, not just a support widget.

**The DONNA Intelligence Network (DIN) is undersold.** The DIN concept — where every DONNA gets smarter as the network grows, without sharing private data — is actually DONNA's most defensible long-term asset and competitive moat. IBM has an entire product line around this concept (watsonx, federated learning, etc.). On DONNA's site it gets one small section. It deserves much more prominent treatment, especially with the "early adopters lock in advantages" framing — because the DIN is *why* that's true.

## **The page title in the browser tab reads "AI Operational Infrastructure for SMBs" — but DONNA isn't targeting SMBs broadly, it's targeting real estate specifically. That's actually a strength, not a limitation. IBM can't say "built for real estate." DONNA can. The specificity is the brand. Consider making the page title, meta description, and any external-facing language consistently say "real estate" rather than SMBs — it signals expertise and filters in exactly the right buyers.**
