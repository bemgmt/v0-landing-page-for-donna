$2M SAFE Memo

Updated Raise Structure and Year-1 Operating Plan

Overview

This memo outlines DONNA’s proposed $2M SAFE raise structure, the ownership implications of each investment tier, and the operating plan tied to each funding level.

The objective is to offer either one investor takes on the full $2M raise or a mix of investors make a clear choice between two check sizes to make the $2M raise, with stronger economics for larger commitments while preserving a cap table that remains healthy for future institutional financing.

The three proposed SAFE options are:

$500,000 SAFE at an $18M valuation cap with a 10% discount

$1,000,000 SAFE at a $15M valuation cap with a 15% discount

$2,000,000 SAFE at a $12M valuation cap with a 20% discount

This structure is designed to reward conviction without creating early dilution that would weaken founder control or complicate later rounds.

\---

Why this structure works

This revised structure is materially stronger than the earlier version.

It gives an investor a meaningful incentive to write a larger check, but it does so within ownership ranges that are still reasonable for a company intended to scale into later venture financing. Rather than forcing an extreme early ownership position, the structure keeps the company investable, preserves future fundraising flexibility, and aligns both founder and investor around long-term enterprise value creation.

In practical terms, it means:

the investor gets better economics at each larger tier

the founder avoids unnecessary early over-dilution

the company retains room for a Seed and Series A

the cap table remains credible to future institutional investors

\---

SAFE conversion framework

These SAFEs include both a valuation cap and a discount. In a future priced equity financing, the SAFE converts at whichever method gives the investor the lower conversion price.

That means the investor receives the benefit of either:

the valuation cap, if the next round is priced well above that cap, or

the discount, if the next round is priced low enough that the discount produces a better effective entry price

This is standard investor-favorable SAFE logic and is the basis for the ownership modeling below.

Break-even valuation points

The break-even valuation is the next priced-round pre-money valuation at which the cap and discount produce the same conversion result. Above that point, the cap is better for the investor. Below that point, the discount is better.

Option	Discount	Cap	Break-even priced round valuation

$1M SAFE	15%	$15M	$17.65M

What changed strategically

The most important improvement is the $2M tier.

Under the old structure, the largest check created an ownership position that was likely too concentrated for a startup expected to pursue future institutional rounds. Under this revised structure, the $2M tier now lands in a range that is still meaningful for the investor while remaining workable for future fundraising.

That is the key advantage of this memo’s structure: it is aggressive enough to attract serious capital, but disciplined enough to support long-term company building.

\---

Cap table implications

This structure keeps the company in a much healthier position after the SAFE conversion.

Approximate founder/common retention before future dilution is:

\~97.22% in the $500K scenario

\~93.33% in the $1M scenario

\~83.33% in the $2M scenario

That leaves room for:

an option pool

a future Seed round

a future Series A

continued founder credibility and control

For future investors, that matters. A company with a clean cap table is easier to fund, easier to govern, and easier to underwrite.

\---

Year-1 operating use by funding level

The three raise sizes are not interchangeable in execution. Each supports a different operating posture.

$500K scenario

This is an MVP and design-partner year.

The focus is on product completion, early customer validation, and building a small number of paying accounts. Team growth remains lean, with reduced founder cash compensation and selective use of fractional support.

Primary goal: prove the product and establish early traction.

$1M scenario

This is a product-plus-early-revenue year.

The company can maintain development velocity, improve onboarding, and begin building a more repeatable customer acquisition process. This scenario supports stronger go-to-market experimentation, but still requires disciplined burn.

Primary goal: show market pull and reach meaningful recurring revenue.

$2M scenario

This is the only scenario that credibly supports a full Year-1 build toward $100K MRR.

It allows DONNA to invest in product, operations, growth, and early sales capacity while preserving enough runway to hit measurable milestones and prepare for the next financing event.

Primary goal: generate clear valuation step-up evidence.

\---

Milestone framing

The raise is tied to execution, not just capital intake.

Across all scenarios, Year 1 is intended to produce:

a working product with real operational depth

paying customers and case-study evidence

improved onboarding and retention

a repeatable sales narrative

a clean KPI story for the next round

In the $2M scenario specifically, the target is to build toward $100K MRR by Month 12, supported by product maturity, customer proof, and an early repeatable go-to-market motion.

\---

Why this is attractive to an investor

The investor is being offered a structure with three advantages.

First, the pricing rewards conviction. A larger check meaningfully improves entry economics.

Second, the structure preserves the company’s long-term ability to scale. That matters because investor upside is strongest when the business remains financable and can continue compounding.

Third, the economics are still substantial. Even under the updated, founder-friendlier terms, the modeled return at a $1B outcome remains highly attractive, especially at the $1M and $2M levels.

This is not a structure built for short-term extraction. It is designed for long-term alignment.

\---

Core pitch

A single investor can take the entire round at $2M or a mix of investors at one of two conviction levels, with increasingly favorable terms tied to check size, while keeping DONNA’s cap table clean enough to scale into future institutional financing.

That is the balance this memo is meant to achieve.

\---

Closing

DONNA is not raising capital to support a vague build cycle. The capital is tied directly to staffing, execution, and measurable business outcomes.

This SAFE structure gives an investor a clear incentive to commit meaningfully, while protecting the company’s ability to grow into a much larger financing story later.

The result is a raise structure that is cleaner, more disciplined, and more strategically durable than the earlier version.
